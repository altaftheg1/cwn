Where we get our news

TheDubaiBrief monitors 29 verified UAE sources — government portals, national newspapers, business media, and safety authorities — every 30 seconds.

Every story on TheDubaiBrief is sourced from an official UAE government body, a nationally recognised newspaper, or a licensed broadcaster. We never use anonymous tips, social media posts, or unverified blogs.

Stories are automatically paraphrased into plain English and checked for calm, factual tone before being published. The original source link is always included so you can read the full story yourself.

How we use these sources

  • Every 30 seconds, TheDubaiBrief automatically checks all 29 sources for new content.
  • Stories are ranked by source tier — government portals are always weighted highest.
  • Headlines and summaries are paraphrased into plain, calm English. Alarmist or sensational language is removed.
  • If a story appears in multiple sources, those sources are listed under the main story card.
  • The original article link is always included — click "Read full story" to go directly to the source.
  • Community reports in the "On The Ground" section are separate — they are resident observations, not source articles, and are clearly labelled.
