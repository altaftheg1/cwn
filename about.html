Our Mission

Living in the UAE means reading news from dozens of different sources, each written for different audiences, in different styles, and often with different levels of alarm. For residents and expats — especially those whose first language isn’t English — it can be hard to know what is actually important and what just sounds scary.

TheDubaiBrief was built to solve that. TheDubaiBrief collects the most important UAE news from trusted official sources and rewrites every story in calm, plain English using AI. No jargon. No clickbait. No panic. Just the facts, clearly explained.

TheDubaiBrief is built and maintained by a student living in Dubai, as a personal project for the community. It is not affiliated with any government, company, or media organisation.

What we stand for
Three pillars
🤔

No Panic

Every headline is rewritten to be calm and factual. We strip out alarming language that adds fear without adding information.

🎯

No Agenda

DUB has no political stance, no commercial interests, and no advertisers. We report what happened, not what someone wants you to think about it.

📖

Just Facts

Every story links back to its original official source. We summarise; the source provides the authority. You can always verify.

Under the hood
How TheDubaiBrief works
1

Collect from official sources

DUB monitors WAM, Gulf News, Khaleej Times, Emirates 24|7, and the UAE Government Portal every 30 minutes for new stories.

2

AI rewrites every headline in plain English

Each article is sent to Claude (Anthropic’s AI) which rewrites the title and summary in simple, calm language that anyone can understand.

3

Full article deep analysis

When you click a story, Claude reads the full article and produces a structured breakdown: key facts, what it means for residents, what happens next, and whether you should be concerned.

4

Community observations

UAE residents can submit short ground observations via “On The Ground.” Every post is AI-moderated before appearing publicly.

5

Original source always linked

Every story displays its source and includes a direct link to the original article. TheDubaiBrief summarises; the source provides the authority.
